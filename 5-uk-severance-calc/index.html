Understanding Statutory Redundancy in the UK (2026)

Being made redundant is a challenging experience, but knowing your legal rights in the UK is the first step toward financial security. For 2026, the statutory weekly pay cap has been adjusted to reflect inflation, ensuring workers receive fair compensation for their years of service. This tool calculates the minimum amount your employer is legally required to pay you, provided you have been with the company for at least two years.

How the Calculation Works

The UK redundancy calculation is unique because it is weighted by age. You receive half a week's pay for each full year you were under 22, one week's pay for each full year you were 22 or older but under 41, and one and a half week's pay for each full year you were 41 or older. This ensures that long-serving, older employees receive a higher safety net during career transitions in 2026.

Is redundancy pay taxable in the UK?

Statutory redundancy pay is usually tax-free up to £30,000. This includes any "enhanced" redundancy pay your employer might offer. However, other payments like notice pay or holiday pay are taxed as normal income.

What if I've worked there for less than 2 years?

Legally, you are not entitled to statutory redundancy pay if you have been employed for less than 2 years. However, you should check your specific employment contract as it may offer "contractual redundancy" benefits.
